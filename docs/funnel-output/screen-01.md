## Screen 1

**URL:** https://www.ethos.com/

### What the user sees

**Hero Section**
Heading: "Protect your family in minutes"
Subtext describing a quick, easy life insurance process. Three trust icons displayed horizontally: "15 minutes" (clock icon), "No medical exam" (checkmark icon), and a third badge (star/award icon). Social proof element showing "4.8★" rating and a user count (e.g., "50,000+ families protected").

**Value Proposition Section**
Heading: "The #1 no medical exam, instant life insurance provider"
Three bullet points with checkmark icons listing key benefits (e.g., instant approval, no exam required, affordable rates).

**Secondary Section**
Heading: "We cover you. You cover them."
Brief supporting copy about coverage for loved ones. A coverage amount or plan visual.

**"Life Insurance made easy" Section**
Step-by-step process breakdown (3–4 steps), each with an icon and short label (e.g., "Answer a few questions," "Get your rate," "Get covered today").

**Free Wills & Trusts Banner**
Highlighted section promoting a free will/trust offer bundled with the policy. Shows a phone mockup image.

**FAQ Section**
Heading: "FAQs"
Several collapsed accordion-style questions about the product.

**"Lock in your best price today" CTA Section**
Repeated call-to-action with a button.

**Footer**
Legal disclaimers, links (Privacy Policy, Terms, etc.), and company information in small text.

---

### What the user can do

- **Primary CTA button** (Hero) — "Get my price" or similar — initiates the quote funnel
- **Secondary CTA button** (bottom of page) — "Get my price" or similar — repeated entry point to the funnel
- **FAQ accordion items** — expandable/collapsible questions; user can click each to reveal the answer
- **Navigation links** (top header) — likely includes logo link (home) and possibly a login or menu link
- **Footer links** — Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, and other legal/informational links
