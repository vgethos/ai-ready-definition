# Q4 '25: Whole Life Primer

## Goals

1. Acquire users who are interested in "Whole Life" and sell them IUL.
2. Communicate that IUL meets their needs better than "Whole Life."

---

## Jobs to Be Done

1. **Ensure my family won't face financial hardship when I die**
   - Primary driver: protect loved ones from financial burden OR give them a leg up to start from
   - Want certainty that coverage will be there when needed
   - Final expenses / funeral costs
   - Medical and/or financial debts

2. **Get guaranteed lifelong coverage that will definitely be there**
   - Security and permanence are paramount
   - Want policy that never expires, never needs renewal
   - Don't want to waste my money with a policy that may not be there

3. **Leave a financial inheritance for my children/family**
   - Beyond covering expenses, want to leave something behind
   - Tax-free death benefit as inheritance

4. **Get predictable premiums and benefits I can count on**
   - Fixed premiums that never increase
   - Known death benefit amounts
   - No surprises or complexity

5. **Secure coverage independent of my employment**
   - Need personal policy not tied to job changes
   - Want control over coverage regardless of career transitions

6. **Find coverage I can actually afford on my budget**
   - Cost is major factor, especially for those on fixed incomes
   - Need transparent pricing upfront

7. **Understand exactly what I'm buying**
   - Want simplicity and transparency
   - Avoid complexity, confusion, or "fine print surprises"

8. **Get coverage that matches what I think "life insurance" should be**
   - Mental model of traditional, straightforward life insurance
   - Familiar product category = trust and comfort

## Why Choose IUL over Whole Life?

| Category | IUL (Indexed Universal Life) | Whole Life Insurance (WL) |
|----------|------------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Cash Value Growth** | Tied to market index (e.g., S&P 500) with cap and floor (e.g., 8.5% cap, 0% floor) | Grows at guaranteed low fixed rate (typically 3–4%) + dividends if paid |
| **Upside Potential** | Higher — market-linked performance, though capped | Limited — lower growth even with dividends |
| **Downside Protection** | Yes — 0% floor, no market losses | Yes — guaranteed growth, no market exposure |
| **Loan Options** | Participating loans — borrowed amount continues earning interest | Non-participating loans — borrowed amount is removed from growth |
| **Income Potential** | Higher — tax-free income via loans with compounding base | Lower — income drains cash value and slows growth |
| **Premium Flexibility** | Flexible — can adjust payments after funding minimums | Rigid — requires fixed premium payments for entire policy life |
| **Cost of Insurance** | Lower in early years, increases with age (requires monitoring) | Level for life, but typically higher upfront costs |
| **Transparency** | Clear index performance and policy cost breakdowns | Often opaque — dividend rates not guaranteed, internal costs hidden |
| **Customization** | Highly customizable — riders, funding, duration can be adjusted | Less customizable — standard policy structure |
| **Legacy Value** | Strong — permanent coverage + potential to grow total DB via Option B | Strong — guaranteed death benefit, fixed growth |

### Why IUL Wins in the Real World

1. **More Growth, Still Safe**
   - IUL gives upside potential without risking principal.
   - WL is safer, but that safety comes with low returns, often not even keeping up with inflation.

2. **Superior Retirement Income Strategy**
   - IUL loans are participating: cash value keeps compounding while you take income.
   - WL loans reduce the balance you're earning on — hurting long-term income potential.

3. **More Flexible for Real-Life Needs**
   - Clients can scale back or pause premiums in IUL (as long as there's cash value).
   - WL is rigid — miss a payment, and you could lapse, unless you've accumulated enough CV.

4. **Better for Tax-Advantaged Income**
   - IUL is often built as a retirement supplement, offering tax-free access and tax-free legacy.
   - WL can provide the same, but usually at a higher cost and lower efficiency.
