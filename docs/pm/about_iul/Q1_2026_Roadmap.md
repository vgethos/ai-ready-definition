# Q1 2026 Roadmap

## Executive Summary

Our IUL growth strategy centers on two distinct paths: scaling net new IUL and Whole Life ad spend, and leveraging IUL to unlock growth in our Term Ads funnel. Both need to scale to achieve our target $20MM in eBITDA.

### Growth Vector 1: IUL Ads Business

Q4 started with slower progress, and it took time to generate wins.

We struggled with efficiency and pulled back from Forbes P1 position to avoid losses. Additionally, aggressive routing to IUL in early Q4 sent some lower-intent users through the funnel who weren't ready to convert, creating post-approval conversion challenges that depressed our ROAS.

We corrected routing mid-quarter and launched top-of-funnel improvements that began showing impact. By December, we started seeing overall recovery: cost per approval fell 30% through product enhancements and Meta creative wins, and CAGB per approval increased 14%. In the past 3 weeks, we've generated higher profit at much lower spend, compared with prior periods. Meta has also emerged as a bright spot, scaling to 1.2X+ pROAS at $39k/week of spend. Finally, we are on the cusp of launching simplified self-serve checkout and instant connect experiences that should produce a step-change in performance.

Almost everything in our Q1 plan centers on this.

### Growth Vector 2: Term Ads Business

We've validated through Q4 experiments that ~30% of users arriving through Term Ads are confused about which product they need or explicitly want permanent life insurance, and these users convert at a much lower rate compared to those who wanted just Term quotes. Merely offering the prospect of quotes for both permanent and term life to these users generated significant interest and drove users deeper in the funnel — we saw a 5% increase in core product lead conversion and approvals. However, transmit rate was negative. In Q1 we'll focus on building the right post-approval digital experience for these 'mixed intent' users to produce a step-change in conversion.

## Strategy: Make IUL Simple to Understand and Exciting to Buy

IUL is a complex product with many different configurations, and lots of insurance jargon. Historically, it's been sold to sophisticated, financially-savvy buyers by agents who showcase their expertise through detailed explanations of policy mechanics — caps, floors, market participation rates, loan provisions, and cash value illustrations. It's a product designed for optimizers who want to understand every variable.

Our challenge is different: **we need to take IUL to the masses, via D2C channels.** The users coming through our funnel aren't financial optimizers. Many have heard of Whole Life, but not IUL, before encountering our ads. They're diving deeply into the concept for the first time in their lives, and they're encountering it in a digital, self-directed environment where they can't ask clarifying questions in real-time until the end.

The fundamental problem: even smart people get lost trying to understand IUL in 5 minutes. We need to build a D2C product that people can grasp in seconds, and get excited about.

### Our Strategy

Our strategy centers on helping everyone — irrespective of permanent protection or accumulation goals, instinctively understand the growth potential of IUL and buy it. We will simply help everyone understand they get 300%–700% more than what they put in and that they can borrow a portion of it.

We are going to stop fine tuning and customizing, and instead selling them the dream of growth. This means two core pillars:

**1. Simplification**
- **Simple, compelling growth story**: A consistent narrative across every touchpoint — onboarding, self-serve, sales, lifecycle — that users can immediately grasp
- **Intuitive language, not jargon**: Leading with concepts people understand naturally, not product seminar terminology like caps, floors, market participation, or crediting mechanisms
- **Standardized SKUs**: Configuring and selling one of two IUL setups to each user (pre-decided based on needs and where they came from), each with clear, compelling, standardized narratives for conveying growth and protection.

**2. Building Genuine Excitement**

At every stage of the journey, we are not just increasing understanding of IUL and how it can meet user goals, but progressively building excitement. At each stage of the funnel we need to get users increasingly convinced of the growth potential of IUL and how it matters to them, and more excitement to get to the next stage of the process.

### What Success Looks Like

Every touchpoint in our flow illustrates these two pillars of simplification and progressive excitement. There's a high degree of cohesion — each stage deliberately builds on the previous one.

- **Creative and landing page**: Users arrive extremely curious and excited to learn more about how IUL can help them grow wealth, or how it may be better than other financial vehicles
- **Onboarding**: Users gain a simple, intuitive understanding of how IUL works and leave excited about how their money grows, and how IUL is the best option for their growth and protection goals. They are eager to get approved and see real numbers.
- **Approval and self-serve**: Users immediately see their personalized growth story — excited by the potential, excited by what they're getting relative to what they put in, and excited for what that growth delivers (retirement income, emergency funds, family legacy). They see clear value in connecting with an agent to finalize
- **Sales**: Agents pick up the momentum, reinforcing the simplified growth story and building on the excitement already established, carrying users smoothly through to purchase
- **Lifecycle**: For users who drop off, re-engagement reinforces the same simplified narrative and reignites excitement, offering another opportunity to complete their purchase

## 1. Onboarding: Establishing Simple Understanding and Initial Excitement

**How we simplify**: We confirm users came for growth and establish their high-level goals in intuitive terms — supplementing retirement income, building family legacy, covering emergencies. We then position IUL in the simplest possible language: you put money in, it grows risk-free with the market, you can use it while living for retirement and other goals, and what you don't use passes to your loved ones. No caps, floors, or crediting mechanisms — just a clear "money in, money out" story.

**How we build excitement**: We get users acknowledging all the ways this growth could enable positive outcomes for them. By exploring their specific goals through engaging UX treatments — drip-wise interstitials, carousels, interactive scenarios — we make the need tangible and position IUL as the perfect solution. Users leave onboarding eager to get approved and see their real numbers.

For the Whole Life audience, we adjust emphasis based on their protection-first mindset, but maintain the same simplified, excitement-driven approach.

## 2. Simplified Checkout That Makes Growth Tangible and Compelling

The post-approval experience builds on the excitement and knowledge acquired during onboarding. Users arrive understanding the basic IUL concept and eager to see their real numbers. Now we show them actual projections — what they put in and what they get for that money in terms of total policy value, death benefit, and accessible cash value — and transform that understanding into intent and excitement to connect with an agent and finalize their purchase.

**How we simplify**:

- *No product configuration*: We'll recommend and sell one of two pre-configured IUL products, each with a clear, standardized narrative for conveying growth and protection. Users don't choose between configurations — we determine which is optimal for them based on basic criteria. This eliminates decision paralysis and ensures we can tell the most compelling growth and protection story for each configuration.
- *Dead simple visualization*: We'll experiment with showing growth in the most intuitive possible visual formats — "What you contribute vs total policy value", "4.6X return", "What you can access over time." Clear, visual representations that anyone can grasp immediately, without needing to read detailed policy mechanics.
- *Answering important questions*: While we address high-level questions during onboarding, consumers may have level 2 questions before they feel confident proceeding — specifics about withdrawing money, the ability to contribute more or pause contributions, and how the product works. We'll use non-invasive UX treatments (inline tooltips, contextual help widgets, floating agent button) to help users get answers without creating friction.
- *Whole Life comparison*: For the Whole Life audience, we build a price comparison experience showing how IUL offers permanent protection and more growth potential than Whole Life, building confidence that they're getting a superior solution.

**How we build excitement**:

With real approved numbers in hand, we amplify the growth story through multiple levers:
- *More aggressive growth scenarios*: Experiment with conservative versus expected versus maximum projections to show the full potential
- *Tangible outcome translation*: Convert growth into concrete benefits using simple statements like "$X per month gives you $Y per month tax-free in retirement"
- *Return on investment emphasis*: Highlight what they're getting relative to what they put in, making the value proposition immediately clear

By the end of self-serve, users are super pumped to connect with an agent to finalize their purchase.

## 3. Instant Connect Handoff: Capitalizing on Peak Excitement

Our North Star is a highly optimized, growth-oriented pseudo self-serve checkout that builds excitement and culminates with a one-click instant connection to an agent. The strategic question: What's the best way to build momentum through self-serve and create an extreme desire to connect with an agent?

**How we simplify**: The connection itself is dead simple — one-click instant handoff. We've built this modularly so it can be plugged into the end of our self-serve flow.

**How we build excitement**: We will experiment with a "pre-approval" positioning strategy borrowed from insights from our sales team. The approach reframes the actual underwriting approval as a "pre-approval" — preliminary numbers that need final verification. We show users their growth projections and get them excited about the potential. The hook to instantly connect becomes: "Connect to see if you qualify for these growth projections."

Behind the scenes, they're already fully approved, but this framing generates urgency and excitement to connect. The moment of reveal happens during the agent call — a moment of surprise and delight — and we carry that momentum forward into the sale.

This pre-approval strategy is our primary bet for maximizing agent connection rates, but we'll explore multiple approaches to find the optimal way to transition users from an exciting self-serve experience into a live conversation.

## 4. Sales and Lifecycle: Reinforcing Simplification and Carrying Momentum Forward

Business hours IUL Approval→transmit rates average ~10%, still well below the 20% we see for Prime approval→transmit CVR on the Term-Ads funnel. Lower connect rates explain only about half the difference. We'll look to exceed Term Ads funnel benchmarks through a) enhanced sales performance and b) re-engaging users with lifecycle campaigns.

### Sales: Carrying Excitement into Purchase

By the time users connect with an agent, they've built substantial momentum. They've been through an onboarding experience that made IUL feel like a no-brainer for their goals. They've seen real numbers that excited them about their growth potential. They've been hooked into instantly connecting with an agent. Now the agent needs to carry that momentum forward into a sale.

- *How we simplify*: Every agent sticks to a highly simplified script with a growth story that directly matches what we told users during onboarding and self-serve. No new concepts, no contradictions — just reinforcement of the compelling narrative they've already bought into. Agents also stick to the same pre-configured IUL setup users already saw in self-serve, maintaining complete consistency from digital experience through to purchase.
- *How we build excitement*: Agents pick up the momentum and carry it forward, using the same simplified growth story to maintain the excitement users arrived with and move them smoothly to purchase.
- *Ensuring consistency*: We achieve this through intensive coaching and Balto scorecards that track adherence in real-time, ensuring every conversation maintains the same quality and messaging.

### Lifecycle: Reinforcing the Story for Drop-Offs

- *How we simplify*: Lifecycle campaigns build on the identical onboarding and self-serve growth story we've already told. We maintain the same simplified language and intuitive framing — no new jargon, just reinforcement of what they've already learned.
- *How we build excitement*: We reinforce the numbers users have seen and get specific about what that growth enables — retirement income, college tuition, emergency funds, family legacy. We address level 2 questions that may have emerged after seeing their numbers. The goal: reinforce the simplified and attractive growth story, answer lingering objections, and hook them once again to instantly connect with an agent to review their policy and check out.

## 5. Meta Optimizations: Applying Simplification and Excitement to Comparison-Driven Users

Meta is our #1 growth channel, and the same core principles of simplification and excitement apply. However, there's one key difference: Meta users arrive via specific comparison hooks that brought them into our funnel. Meta's best-performing creatives use comparison formats — IUL versus 401(k), S&P 500, and CDs. These comparisons resonate because they address the core question Meta users have: "Is IUL the best place to put my money for my financial goals?"

Our first priority is nailing the channel-agnostic growth story — selling IUL compellingly across onboarding, self-serve checkout, and sales for all audiences. Once we're on firm footing there, we layer in Meta-specific optimizations that acknowledge and build on the comparison hook that brought users in.

**How we simplify**: We maintain simplification by keeping the comparison framework consistent throughout the journey. Landing pages explicitly acknowledge how users arrived — meeting them where they are rather than forcing an immediate pivot. The growth-oriented onboarding story adapts to showcase IUL's advantages over the specific instrument they're comparing in simple terms: for 401(k) comparisons, emphasizing downside protection from market drops while maintaining upside; for S&P 500 comparisons, highlighting tax-free growth and downside protection.

**How we build excitement**: Post-approval, we build comparison experiences similar to our IUL versus Whole Life treatment — but for IUL versus 401(k), IUL versus S&P 500, etc. With the benefit of real approved numbers, we show how IUL projects to perform relative to alternative investments over time, making the superiority concrete and exciting. Sales and lifecycle also reinforce these comparisons, with scripts and email content that use their actual numbers to demonstrate IUL's superiority for their specific situation.

This creates a seamless, consistent narrative from ad creative through purchase — acknowledging the comparison hook that brought them in while building excitement about IUL's advantages.

## Initiative 6: Cross-Product Sales to Dropped-Off Consumers

Many customers won't purchase the first product we recommend. We'll make our best assessment of their needs and recommend IUL configured for accumulation, IUL positioned as whole life replacement, or Term life. But sometimes we'll get it wrong, or the (IUL) price point will be too high, or their priorities will shift after seeing real numbers. Our re-engagement strategy maximizes conversion by cross-pollinating users between different products and product experiences when they don't purchase initially.

If a user doesn't purchase the primary recommended product within N days of their initial approval, we'll assess why and offer an alternative that addresses their likely objection. For users who saw an IUL whole-life-centric experience but didn't convert, we might pivot to Term as a lower-cost option to protect loved ones during critical years, acknowledging that the IUL price point may have been too high. For users who saw a Term experience but didn't convert, we might pivot to IUL as a lifetime protection option with guaranteed inheritance, addressing the concern that Term "expires" and leaves nothing behind. We'll define multiple re-engagement experiences — one for users seeing each initial product recommendation and being redirected to another.

**Example**: A user arrives through Whole Life ads seeking permanent protection and sees our pseudo self-serve IUL experience emphasizing legacy and guaranteed inheritance. The monthly premium is $300, and they don't convert. After 3 days, we send SMS and email: "Still want to protect your family? Get the same death benefit coverage for $100/month with Term life insurance — perfect for covering your mortgage and protecting your family during their most vulnerable years." We direct them to a self-serve Term funnel where they can complete their purchase at a price point that works for their budget.

## B. Using IUL to Grow Our Term Ads Business

The Term Ads funnel represents the biggest near term opportunity to grow eBITDA from IUL. Our Q1 experiments validated that up to 30% of Term Ads funnel users are interested in obtaining both Term and Permanent Life offers. These mixed-intent users convert at half the rate of clear-intent Term users. Simply offering the prospect of quotes for both products drove a 5% increase in lead conversion and approvals — proof that addressing non-Term curiosity or intent can drive growth. We did not, however, see these approval gains hold to transmit, leading to a major untapped opportunity.

The strategic challenge is creating post-approval experiences that confidently drive mixed-intent users toward the right decision. We'll test multiple approaches: one that leans toward Term while de-prioritizing permanent, and another that leans toward permanent while de-prioritizing Term.

The key philosophy is positioning ourselves as experts who make recommendations based on user needs, while "showing our work" — displaying attributes of both products in a way that allows comparison if users want to evaluate the alternative.

This achieves multiple goals simultaneously: it resolves curiosity about the non-recommended product, educates genuinely unsure users about the key differences, builds confidence in our recommendation by demonstrating we've considered their options thoughtfully, and provides a clear path to purchase the alternative if they truly want permanent life. Users leave feeling informed and confident rather than confused and paralyzed.
