# What's an IUL?

> Goal: HMW explain IUL to anyone, in 3 sentences — and HMW we sell this more effectively.

---

## How to Explain It

**Paragraph version**

IUL is an innovative life insurance policy that combines the benefits of a traditional whole life insurance policy (with permanent protection starting on day 1) with an investment vehicle that significantly grows the value of your policy over time. This growth is market-based, with a cap of 8.5% and a floor of 0%. As an added benefit, you can take loans against a portion of your policy while you're alive.

**Short version**

Permanent life insurance that grows steadily with the market. It not only provides a guaranteed payout to your family starting on day one, but builds cash you can tap into at any time.

**1-sentence version**

IUL is permanent life insurance that grows with the market and builds cash you can use while alive.

| **Trade off** | Less cash growth | Lower death benefit long-term | Lower death benefit to start |

### Level DB (A)

With a level death benefit, your family's payout is locked in from day one and stays the same for the life of the policy. Your cash value still grows over time, but it's essentially separate — it's yours to use while alive, not something that adds to what your family receives. This option is best for people whose primary goal is guaranteeing a specific inheritance amount, and who see the cash value as a personal safety net rather than part of their legacy.

### B (Increasing) → A (Level)

With an increasing death benefit, your death benefit grows alongside your cash value — meaning early on, your family receives both. Over time, the policy converts to a level death benefit, and the death benefit flattens out. This option works well for people who want flexibility: during the increasing phase, the cash value is "going to family anyway," so accessing it feels less like you're taking from them. The trade-off is that your day-one protection is lower, and the long-term death benefit may end up smaller than a pure level policy.

## FAQs

### Whole Life

- **Is my coverage guaranteed?**
  - Yes, as long as you pay your monthly premium, it is guaranteed for life.

- **When does it start? When does it end?**
  - Your coverage starts when you activate your policy (that can be today). It only ends when you pass away, or if you stop paying the monthly premium.

- **What is covered? Is the payout tax-free?**
  - The inheritance can be used for anything by your beneficiaries (mortgage costs, tuition help, etc). And yes, it is tax-free.

- **Can I access my policy while alive?**
  - Yes, your policy includes funds that grow that you can use through loans and withdrawals.

- **What type of policy am I buying?**
  - Your policy is called Indexed Universal Life. It's designed not to decrease as long as the policy stays active, and it will be paid to your beneficiaries tax-free.

### IUL → Level DB

- **How does my cash value grow?**
  - Your cash value grows tax-free with the S&P 500 index, up to 8.5% annually. And with a 0% floor, you're protected from losses if the market dips. The projection above is based on a 5.56% rate.

- **How and when can I access my cash value?**
  - You can start taking tax-free loans once you've earned enough cash value, usually by year 3 of your policy.

- **What will my family receive?**
  - Your family enjoys lifelong protection — starting on day one. They'll get a guaranteed amount of $[351,957], tax-free. Your cash value does not go to your loved ones after you die. If you don't pay back what you borrow from your cash value, it will be deducted from the amount they receive.

- **Are there fees for accessing cash value?**
  - There are no fees to take a loan, but there is an interest rate, typically 3.5–5%. However, your full cash value amount continues growing even if you take out a loan.
