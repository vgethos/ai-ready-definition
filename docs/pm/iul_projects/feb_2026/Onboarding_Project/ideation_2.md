## What We're Solving For

The core claim — "what your family receives is typically 3–4x what you put in" — moves from Screen 2 body copy to Screen 3, where it gets a full screen to land. Copy in progress; visualization deferred.

2. **The cash value reveal (Screens 8–9) — scope expanded.** *(2/18 decision)* The market-linked growth and loss protection concept (previously Screen 3) now folds into cash value education. Screens 8–9 must carry: cash value concept introduction, market-linked growth, loss protection (the floor), and the "access while alive" story. A third screen in this section may be needed — revisit after Screen 3 is locked.

3. **The chapter screen before PII (Screen 10).** Still open. Screen 10 in the current designs ("Great — we'll show you your growth potential in a few minutes!") does some of this work, but the payoff feels vague and the shift in mode isn't clearly marked. What does a sharper version say?

## Principles

*Carried forward from ideation_1, updated for the compliance constraints and Q1 strategic direction.*

**Compliance is load-bearing, not an afterthought.** Death benefit copy does not get market language. Cash value does not get described as guaranteed. "Returns" is gone — use "payout" for what the family receives. Every claim uses "could" not "will."

**Additive, not competitive.** Cash value is a discovery the user makes after the death benefit story has landed. It never reframes or competes with the inheritance story. It adds to it.

**One idea per screen.** *(2/18: the combined growth + protection exception is retired. Screen 3 is now the ROI screen — one idea. Market-linked growth and loss protection fold into cash value education on Screens 8–9.)* Every screen gets one job.

**The family is always the subject.** The user is building something for the people they love. Cash value access is framed as something the policyholder can tap *without taking from their family* — not as a personal wealth story that's disconnected from protection.

**Earned complexity.** Mechanics (floors, caps, loan interest) belong in footnotes. A screen earns the right to introduce cash value mechanics only after the death benefit story has fully landed.

**Sell the growth story simply.** Per Q1 direction: the goal is not to teach the full policy structure. It's to make the idea of 3–5x growth feel real, safe, and exciting. Stop fine-tuning; sell the dream.

## The Wealth Frame — Decided

**Wealth = what your loved ones receive.** The death benefit is the primary growth story. The core message: every dollar a policyholder puts in could leave behind significantly more for their family — typically 3–5x. That's the ROI. That's what grows. Cash value is a bonus discovery layered on top, never the lead.

This decision cascades into copy throughout the flow:

- **Screen 2:** IUL "does both" = lifelong protection *and* growth for your family. The growth story is about what they receive, not what you accumulate. *(2/18: the 3–4x claim moves to Screen 3.)*
- **Screen 3:** *(2/18: reconceived)* The ROI screen. Makes the 3–4x multiplier feel real and tangible. No market language — this is a death benefit story.
- **Screens 8–9:** Cash value lands as a bonus — something the policyholder can access *without reducing what goes to the family*. *(2/18: now also carries market-linked growth and loss protection.)*

Three reasons this is the right frame:

1. **Traffic source alignment.** The ads driving this funnel lead with family protection and growth *for* the family. A self-wealth frame would be a tonal break on arrival. Consistency from ad to onboarding matters.

2. **Compliance is cleaner.** Keeping cash value in a secondary role means market-linked language stays in footnotes where it belongs, not in prominent headlines.

3. **The data.** 78% of IUL users express interest in protection. The largest purchase segment said "both" and wanted permanent coverage. The death-benefit-first frame reaches more of the funnel.

> A self-wealth frame (wealth = what the policyholder can access while alive) may be right for a specific sub-segment — accumulation-intent users, or those who select "Growing my own wealth" on Screen 1. Worth exploring as a personalized branch, not the default.

### Screen 3 — The ROI Screen *(education — reconceived 2/18 — 3 variants)*

> **Previous variants 3A / 3B / 3C are retired.** Those were built around market-linked growth and loss protection — concepts that now belong in cash value education (Screens 8–9). Screen 3's new job: make the death benefit ROI feel real and tangible. The multiplier is introduced here for the first time; Screen 2 sets it up without stating the number.

| | 3A | 3B | 3C |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Concept** | The Number Does the Work | The Reveal | The Relational Frame |
| **Eyebrow** | THE GROWTH STORY | HERE'S HOW MUCH | WHAT THEY RECEIVE |
| **Headline** | "What your family receives could be 3–4x what you paid in." | "For every dollar you put in, your family could receive 3–4x." | "You build it. They could receive 3–4x what you put in." |
| **Body** | "That gap between what you pay and what they receive — that's what you're building." | "Most people don't realize how much more their family receives than what was paid in. That's the point." | "Every payment you make grows what your family could one day receive — tax-free." |
| **Visual** | TBD (deferred) | TBD (deferred) | TBD (deferred) |
| **Footnote** | Death benefit payout is tax-free and varies based on age, health, and coverage amount selected. Typical multiplier of 3–4x reflects an illustrative range; actual results will vary. | Same as 3A | Same as 3A |
| **CTA** | Next | Next | Next |

**3A: Explanation**
States the claim plainly and lets it land. The body reframes the multiplier emotionally — "that gap... that's what you're building" — without adding information. Cleanest compliance path: "could," no market language. The risk: it's the least surprising of the three. Might feel like a fact rather than a revelation.

**3B: Explanation**
The eyebrow ("HERE'S HOW MUCH") primes the user for a number before the headline delivers it. The body leans into the surprise element — "most people don't realize" — which validates the reaction the user is likely having. The risk: "most people don't realize" may read as a sales technique to a skeptical user.

**3C: Explanation**
Explicitly separates actor from receiver — "you build it, they receive it" — which is the emotional core of the whole flow. "Tax-free" does double duty: it's a real benefit and a memorability cue. The risk: "you build it" implies an active construction metaphor that the rest of the flow doesn't sustain.

**9A: Explanation**
"Did you know?" frames cash value as a bonus discovery rather than a sales beat. The user has already committed to the protection story — this is the moment they learn there's more, and it lands as a gift, not a pitch. The payoff screen makes it tangible by personalizing to their stated goal.

**9B: Explanation**
Users who opt in are primed — their engagement with the cash value story should be stronger. The risk: we lose the cash value story entirely for skippers, and we don't know if that hurts transmit rate. This is a test, not a default. Hypothesis: primed users convert at a high enough rate to offset the skippers.

**9C: Explanation**
Cleanest execution of the "earned complexity" principle — growth concept first, access concept second, one idea each. The trade-off is screen count. Only worth it if data shows screen-level drop-off isn't the problem.
