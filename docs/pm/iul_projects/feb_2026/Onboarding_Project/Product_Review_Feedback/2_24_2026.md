# IUL Onboarding Design Review – Meeting Summary
**Figma:** [IUL BoF Onboarding Q1 '26](https://www.figma.com/design/f75htHnoN0TGyLU780HZMH/)

---

## Core Issue: The Two Variants Are Too Similar

The meeting centered on a fundamental question: do the two onboarding variants (inheritance/death benefit vs. cash value) need to exist as separate flows? The consensus was that the variants currently feel like internal distinctions rather than meaningfully different user experiences. Prassath's test: show them to 3 people and they'd struggle to spot the difference.

**Decision:** Nail the inheritance variant first with all feedback incorporated, then evaluate whether a truly differentiated cash value variant is warranted.

---

## Terminology: "Wealth" vs. "Inheritance"

- In this variant, **wealth = inheritance** — be deliberate and consistent with this throughout all materials
- "Wealth" is the primary term (especially on the landing page and ad creative); "inheritance" serves as the supporting/explanatory word
- Jeff to add screenshots of ad creative and landing page alongside designs to audit consistency — Facebook is the #1 channel by spend, so alignment there is especially critical

---

## Most Critical Design Problem: ROI Visualization on the First Green Screen

This was identified as the make-or-break moment of the entire flow.

- The **3-5X message is not landing visually** — the connection between "3 to 5X" in the headline and the bar graph isn't immediately obvious
- The black line in the current exploration is being missed; users can't tell at a glance they'd get 5x what they put in
- **Prassath prefers the bar chart over the line graph** for showing ROI — it's cleaner and more direct
- Ash wants the right bar to be so obviously larger than the left bar that you "can't miss the point"
- Prassath suggested adding a connecting line with a circle between the bars to reinforce the comparison
- Ash suggested **consolidating into a single screen** rather than splitting across two
- Language note: consider **"3 to 5 times"** instead of "3 to 5X" for a more conversational feel; stick with this framing (not 300–500%) due to compliance concerns around implying "returns"

---

## Headlines vs. Subtext

Prassath's overarching critique: the real insight is buried in subtext while headlines read like generic marketing copy.

- Deliver the punch **in the headline itself**, even if it runs four lines
- Example: "With IUL, your family gets 3 to 5 times what you put in — and they're covered from day one" is stronger than simply saying "both"
- Every screen should be evaluated: if the most interesting thing on it is in the subtext, rewrite the headline

---

## The Two Variants: Key Differentiators & Feedback

| | Inheritance Variant | Cash Value Variant |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary message** | 3-5X return for family | Tax-free cash access during life |
| **8.5% growth rate** | ❌ Compliance issue | ✅ Can feature prominently |
| **Tax benefits** | Secondary | Primary |
| **Current problem** | Cash value comes too late in flow | Feels like a "tax benefit version," not truly cash value-focused |

- The tax comparison slide (multiple calculations) is **too cognitively complex** — "too much math going on"
- The simpler **"borrow $150K tax-free for 3 things"** message lands far better
- The cash value variant needs to lean more heavily into the term "cash value" itself and make that concept clearer upfront

---

## The Case for Consolidation

Prassath proposed weaving everything into one strong inheritance variant:
1. Open with the **3-5X bar chart**
2. Add the **8.5% growth / 0% downside** message on the second screen ("how do you want your money to grow")
3. Include **cash value messaging** on the following screen

**Ash's concern:** Combining 8.5% growth AND 3-5X in one variant risks confusion — they're two different ways of thinking about growth. This needs precise wording and compliance sign-off from Adrian and Troy before proceeding.

---

## Language: "Cash Value" Directly

Juan raised whether they should just use the term "cash value" explicitly:
- At least half of users in transcripts are already asking about it by name
- Could simplify compliance framing (e.g., "the cash value grows at 8.5%")

**Team response:** It's less about the industry term and more about clear language — **"cash from your IUL"** or **"borrow cash value tax-free"** are both acceptable. Don't test terminology — identify what's clearest and commit to it.

---

## Risk Flag (Jeff)

The inheritance variant doesn't mention cash value until later in the flow. Since Meta ads promise money users can access during their lifetime, not echoing that message early creates **drop-off risk**. Ash acknowledged this but noted that mentioning cash value later is "significantly less risky" than not mentioning it at all — which he called a past mistake.

---

## Three Universal Messages (Every Variant Should Hit These)

1. **3-5X return** — the primary value proposition
2. **Tax-free cash borrowing** — money accessible during life
3. **8.5% growth with 0% downside** — money goes up, never goes down

## Action Plan

**Step 1 — Refine the Inheritance Variant:**
- Incorporate all meeting feedback (especially ROI visualization)
- Make 3-5X visually unmissable on the first green screen
- Attempt to weave in 8.5% growth on the second screen with precise, compliance-safe wording
- Identify risks in the refined variant
- Explore forks: e.g., current framing vs. a version that routes some users to term insurance

**Step 2 — Evaluate the Cash Value Variant:**
- Only after the team feels confident in the inheritance variant
- Assess whether a second variant is truly differentiated enough to warrant a separate test
- Ash to huddle with team to make this call
