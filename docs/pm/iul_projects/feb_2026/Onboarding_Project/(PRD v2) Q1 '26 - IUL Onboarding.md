## Context and Data

### Summary of learnings and observations

**1. RE Intent Questions:**

**a. Raw data**
- 65% of IUL users say they want both protection and growth.
  - Conversion is great for the half of this group that says they want lifelong coverage.
- 22% say they mostly want growth.
- 13% say they mostly want protection.
- That means 78% are interested in at least protection.
- That means 87% of users are interested in at least growth.

**b.** The initial intent question frames the conversation as if there are three dimensions/outcomes "growth, protection, or both", but we know that the three outcomes are actually "Term, protection focused IUL, accumulation focused IUL."

As a result, it's not surprising that there is very little correlation between user preferences for "growth, protection, or both" and the IUL configuration they end up buying. The fact that user outcomes are unrelated to the inputs is evidence that we are not telling a cohesive story from start to end.

**c. Coverage duration does not predict product selection**
- i. "Both" users who are unsure about coverage duration overwhelmingly self-select into IUL. This suggests that if you want growth at all, you want a permanent policy.
- ii. "Protection" users who are unsure about coverage duration evenly split between IUL and Term. This suggests they are indexing on something else, like growth.

**2.** Budget questions do not sufficiently predict on their own whether a user will buy an IUL or Term policy.
- a. Users with "low" budgets convert just as well for IUL, suggesting that intent and education play a larger factor than cost.

## Project Hypotheses

### Primary

1. More users will be more bought into IUL and thus will answer our calls more often and transmit more often if they are excited to apply / buy.
2. Users will be excited to apply / buy if they learn the following:
   - a. They understand that IUL offers lifelong protection + a way to grow money to use while alive
   - b. That this is a great way to grow wealth (market linked, tax free, loss protection, high ROI)
   - c. This growth enables them to do exciting things they care about WHILE ALIVE (retirement, emergencies, college savings, etc.)
   - d. The protection enables them to do a, b, and c
3. Users will be more excited to apply/buy if what they learn about IUL aligns with their needs.

## Goal

Improve conversion from approval to transmit by improving top of funnel education and improving user buy-in to our product recommendation.

## User Goals

Primary goals for users who start the IUL funnel:

1. Learn more about what IULs are and how Ethos IULs work
2. Determine if Ethos IULs will meet their insurance/investment goals
3. Determine how much it will cost to get an Ethos IUL and what they can accomplish specifically for that price

## Key Messaging

*(What users understand and believe from this experience)*

### Ideal messaging/value props for IUL:

**Strong growth:**
- Grows with the stock market (up to 8.5%)
- OR, It can pay you back 3x what you invest
- OR, stronger returns than WL
- Better growth than the best savings account
- Steady, safe returns with the market
- Related: Invest more, when you have extra cash, to turbocharge your growth

**Loss protection:**
- Safe from market losses with a built-in 0% floor
- Don't lose sleep with a built-in floor to protect you from market losses
- Get the market gains without the market losses
- No matter what happens in the market, you're safe

**Accessing your cash value:**
- Using the money – tax free
  - Save XX compared to if you had to pay taxes
  - Contribute more than a 401k, take it out earlier tax-free
  - Likewise: Like a 401k, without all of the red tape.
  - Loan yourself money AND keep it growing
- When you borrow money, your wealth still grows with the market
- "Be your own bank (Infinite banking)"
- Use it for retirement, college, etc.

**Lifelong protection:**
- Your family gets lifelong coverage, from day 1
- Your family's protection grows over time.
- Cover mortgage, tuition, living expenses

**Other topics:**
- Lowering contributions
- The policy can pay for itself with accumulated value
- Comparison against traditional whole life insurance - why IUL is better (grows with your investment)

### Idea 1: Collect Needs First, Then Educate

**Hypothesis:**
By asking users about their needs and tying the product recommendation to those needs, we can get more customers to get approved, answer the phone (connect), and transmit.

The greatest differentiators between users who want term and users who want IUL is growth, followed by the idea of permanent coverage.

The value of education will succeed more or less equally in this experiment, therefore the winning variant will be the one that:
1. Makes the education feel natural and worth completing.
2. Most successfully identifies and diverts the 20% of users who want term.

**Risk(s):**
1. Users may drop off mid-education because they don't know how long it is.
2. Users may realize they prefer a different product after they go through education.

[Figma (1/20)](#)

#### Idea 1 Experience

##### STEP 1: INTENT

1. Ask the user about protection / who depends on them financially
   - **Questions:**
     - Ask the user who they want to protect
       - If they said they have children, ask them how many [over 18]
     - Ask the user what they want to protect their family from
   - **Context:**
     - These two questions perform well today.
     - We know that ~80% of IUL users express some interest in protection.

2. Ask the user if they want growth from their life insurance policy (with "Unsure" option)
   - **Context:**
     - We hypothesize that this will be a great way to differentiate users interested in IUL from users who are interested in term.
     - We can use this info to fork the user experience accordingly.
   - **Ideas:**

     **Option A: Behavioral** – "Which sounds like a better fit?" *(alt: "What are your goals for life insurance?")*
     - I want my life insurance to double as an investment that grows over time
     - I want to protect my family at the lowest cost, while they are most vulnerable
     - Help me decide

     **Option B: Direct** – "Do you want your life insurance to grow money you can use later?"
     - No, I'm just interested in protection at the lowest cost
     - Yes, I'd like to invest through my policy
     - Help me decide

     **Option C: Intent** – "What are your goals for life insurance?"
     - I want to protect my [children] and grow money I can use
     - I want to protect my [children] for the lowest price
     - Help me decide

##### STEP 2: EDUCATION ROUTING

**A.** If they are **UNSURE** about whether they want growth, educate them on the difference between term and IUL and let them choose which one they want.
- See Figma for education experience

**B.** If they **WANT GROWTH**, route to IUL education
- (Optional) There is an opportunity to engage these users with questions that will inform future copy/story or even product config. We should discuss which ones are most important for us to know.
- For example:
  - i. Should we emphasize cash value growth or death benefit growth?
  - ii. Do users prefer "Level DB" or "Increasing to Level" death benefits?
    - Context: Today, we sell these in a roughly 40/60 split, respectively. This suggests we have two groups whom we can speak to with different value props.

**C.** If they **WANT LOW COST PROTECTION**, route to Term education.

##### Step 3a: UNSURE EDUCATION
*Context: User has indicated they aren't sure if they want growth*

**Approach:**
1. Screen 1: Introduce the user to the idea that they have two options to choose from and what features to focus on. (cost, coverage length, growth, etc.)
2. Screen 2: Teach the user about term.
   - i.e: Low cost/high value, for a specific time period, excels at protecting family while they are most vulnerable
3. Screen 3: Teach the user about the key features of an IUL
   - Grows cash that you can access cash tax free while alive.
   - Permanent/Lifetime Protection for family
     - Can also say it grows overtime if we assume we're selling "increasing to level"
   - We should position cost as an investment, not something you can buy. In other words, it "costs" as much as you want to put in. You wouldn't say it "costs" money when you put money in a 401k, would you?
4. Screen 4: Let the user choose between IUL and Term

##### Step 3b: IUL EDUCATION
*Context: User has indicated they want growth.*

**Approach:**
1. Screen 1: IUL sounds like a great fit! [tie to expressed user needs]
2. Screen 2: [talk about growth and downside protection]
3. Screen 3: [talk about cash value access]
4. Screen 4: [talk about permanent protection]

Build excitement with growth-focused CTA (i.e. See my growth potential)

##### Step 3c: TERM EDUCATION
*Context: User has indicated they are NOT interested in growth and/or that they prefer low cost protection*

**Approach:** Show the term NAP

##### Step 4: MODIFIED PRE-INTERVIEW
*Chaptering story: "Now that we know what you want, let's get you a rate"*

Alt: Let's get you your [real] growth rate/potential

**Chaptering:**
1. Confirm eligibility
   - Includes the question "When would you like to be covered by health insurance"
   - Includes the question "Provide your sex at birth"
2. Provide health and lifestyle info
3. Get your final rate
   - Alt: Get my [real] growth rate/potential

**Remove these screens:**
1. Do you have an estate plan or Will in Place?
   - What data do we have on the value of this?
   - If valuable, add into educational flow
     - i.e. after screen 4: Bonus: All Ethos policies come with free Will/estate planning, free of charge!
2. NAP (we already did this in the education)

### Idea 2: Education First (no intent)

**Context:**
Users are coming to Ethos from IUL-specific ads.

How do we transition someone from an ad to this flow?

**Hypothesis:**
1. If we start users with IUL education, we don't need intent questions because they will decide for themselves if that product makes sense for them. In other words, if they don't drop off, they are already bought into the product we are going to sell them.
2. We need to transition users from the ad into education. Starting directly with education is too abrupt of a jump.
   - We can do this through a fun fact, greeting, or even a simple question.

**Risk(s):**
1. Leading with too many education screens may bore users who did not sign up for a lecture of indeterminate length.
2. The >20% of users who would prefer Term will "realize" that IUL is not a fit and drop off before seeing any option to continue with term.

[Figma (1/20)](#)
- Start with myth/fact question (alt)
- Start with greeting
- Start with fun fact

---

### Idea 3: Mix Education and Intent

[Figma (1/20)](#)

**Hypothesis:**
1. If we start the pre-interview with too many education screens, users will drop off because it's not engaging.
2. If we sprinkle education and intent together, users can stay engaged and learn about the product along the way.

**Risk:**
1. Term users won't successfully identify the early offramp and drop off during the rest of the education.
2. Bringing pre-interview questions into the education flow may feel like too much of a commitment too early on.

---

### Idea 4: New NAP

[Figma (1/20)](#)

**Hypothesis:**
1. The current intent questions are optimal and should not be changed.
2. The bulk of the value we'll get from this experiment is by adding educational content to the IUL NAP experience.

**Risks:**
1. Users will continue to get the potentially wrong product recommendation.
2. Users will not be bought into the recommendation because of mismatches between what they think they want and our recommendation.
